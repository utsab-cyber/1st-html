Red Fort

The Red Fort> is a historic fort located in Delhi, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648.

The fort is made of red sandstone, which gives it its name. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for many years.

The Red Fort is an important symbol of India’s history and independence. Every year on Independence Day, the Prime Minister hoists the national flag here.

It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts thousands of visitors from all over the world.
